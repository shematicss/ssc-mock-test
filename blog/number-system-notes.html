Number System — Concise Notes & Practice

Divisibility, remainders, LCM/HCF and last-digit tricks for SSC exams.

Notes · Quant

Reading time: ~7 min · Updated:

1) Divisibility rules (quick)

NumberRule
2Last digit even
3Sum of digits divisible by 3
4Last two digits divisible by 4
5Last digit 0 or 5
8Last three digits divisible by 8
11|(sum odd places − sum even places)| divisible by 11

2) Remainders (modular basics)

Key: If a ≡ b (mod m), then you can replace a by b in any multiplication/addition mod m.

Trick: For large powers, reduce base first. Example: Find last digit of 7^123. Cycle of last digit of 7 → 7,9,3,1 (length 4). 123 ≡ 3 (mod 4) ⇒ last digit is 3.

3) LCM & HCF (prime power rule)

Write prime powers: LCM → take higher powers; HCF → lower powers.

NumbersLCMHCF
60 (=2²·3·5) & 90 (=2·3²·5)2²·3²·5 = 1802·3·5 = 30

4) Last-digit / last-two-digit tricks

  • Last digit of a product depends only on last digits of factors.
  • Use cycles for bases ending with 2,3,7,8.
  • For last two digits, use mod 100 and Chinese Remainder with 4 & 25 if needed.

5) Practice set

  1. Find remainder when 5^123 is divided by 7.
  2. LCM of 48, 60 and 90.
  3. Last two digits of 13^7.
  4. Smallest number divisible by 12, 15 and 20 leaving remainder 3 each.
Exam tip: Maintain a 1-page formula sheet. Revise it daily and mark weak topics after each mock.
